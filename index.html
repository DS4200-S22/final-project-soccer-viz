Expected Performance In European Football Leagues

Henry Dench, Forrest Meng, Sarah Casale

Course Project as part of DS4200 S22: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Ab Mosca, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

With the help of modern data collection and machine learning, new ways of quantifying the game of soccer have come to the forefront. One of these metrics, called xG, quantifies the number of goals a team or player are expected to score based on the quality of chances they have. Our project aim is to use this new and exciting statistic to provide both a high-level visualization of how teams performed, along with more specific player-based information of the past five seasons.

Visualization Explanation

On the left is a scatter plot that represents performance data for teams. Points on the scatter plot represent different teams. There is also a dotted line, (xG = xG Conceded), that runs diagonally across the scatter plot. If a team is above this line, they are creating more/better goal scoring chances than they are conceding. When hovering the mouse on a team point, a tool-tip will appear that displays the season and team name, along with the following data: xG per 90, xGc per 90, G per 90, Gc per 90, along with the record of the team over that season. The visualization on the right side is a bar graph that is linked to the scatter plot. The bar chart reflects the burshing chosen by the user on the scatterplot If the user selects a team/teams in the scatter plot, the bar graph will be populated with player data from that respective team and season combo. The bar plot will display the top 30 xG per 90 players of the selected data on the scatter plot. Lastly, when a user hovers over a bar, a tool-tip will appear with player data such as name, position, nationality, and performance statistics such as G per 90, xG per 90, A per 90, xA per 90, and 90s played.
